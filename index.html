Introduction

A microscope is an instrument used to magnify small objects that are not visible to the naked eye. It allows scientists, researchers, and students to study the structure and details of microscopic specimens.

Types of Microscopes

  • Compound Microscope
  • Stereoscope
  • Electron Microscope
  • Scanning Probe Microscope
  • Confocal Microscope

Usage

Microscopes have a wide range of applications in various fields such as biology, medicine, materials science, forensics, and more. They are used to examine cells, microorganisms, tissues, crystals, and other small objects.

Maintenance

To ensure the optimal performance of a microscope, regular maintenance is required. This includes cleaning the lenses, adjusting the focus, and proper storage to prevent dust accumulation. It's also important to handle the microscope with care to avoid damage.
